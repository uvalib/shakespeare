Shakespeare by the Book

Four Centuries of Printing, Editing, and Publishing

February 22 - December 31, 2016

The University of Virginia presents an exhibition to celebrate the vibrant, complex, and sometimes contentious history of bringing Shakespeare's works into print. From early editions to contemporary artists' books, and from massive folios to microminiature editions, the exhibition offers visitors a glimpse into the varied riches of the Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library's Shakespeare collections. Installed in our main gallery, the exhibition includes over 100 items.

"Shakespeare by the Book" was produced by the Harrison-Small Research Center, University of Virginia Library. Unless otherwise indicated, items on display are from the holdings of the Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library. From October 1-26, 2016, some items in this exhibition will be relocated to the First Floor Gallery to make room for the visiting Folger Shakespeare Library exhibition, First Folio! The Book That Gave Us Shakespeare. These items will be returned to the gallery upon the Folio's departure.


Special thanks to Ralph Cohen, Victor Luftig, Katharine Maus, and David Vander Meulen, who spearheaded the UVA English Department's successful grant application to the American Library Association to bring a visiting First Folio to UVA.

First Folio! The Book that Gave Us Shakespeare.

Courtesy of the Folger Library

October 1st – 26th 2017

“Shakespeare by the Book” will become the backdrop for the Folger Shakespeare Library exhibition featuring the celebrated First Folio of 1623. First Folio! The Book That Gave Us Shakespeare is a national traveling exhibition to commemorate the 400th anniversary of Shakespeare's death. The First Folio contains 36 of Shakespeare's plays, 18 of which were never printed before.

In conjunction with the First Folio visit, there will be a First Folio! Family Day on October 8, 11 a.m.–2:00 p.m. on the lawn in front of the Harrison Institute/Small Special Collections Library and Alderman Library.

Also, on October 12, at 4:00 p.m., the Tracy W. and Katherine W. McGregor Distinguished Lecture in American History will be given by Virginia Mason Vaughan, Professor Emerita, Clark University, on the topic of “Shakespeare for the American People,” in the auditorium of the Albert and Shirley Small Special Collections Library.
